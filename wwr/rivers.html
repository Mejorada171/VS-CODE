Main Fork of the Salmon River, Idaho

The Main Fork of the Salmon River is a major river located in central Idaho, United States. It is also known as the "River of No Return" due to its rugged and remote nature. The river runs through the heart of the Frank Church River of No Return Wilderness Area, which is the largest wilderness area in the continental United States. The Main Fork of the Salmon River is approximately 84 miles long, and it flows through a deep canyon with steep cliffs and narrow gorges. The river is known for its clear water, which is ideal for fishing and other recreational activities as whitewater rafting and kayaking.

Desolayton and Gray Canyon Utah

Desolation Canyon is a remote canyon located in eastern Utah, carved by the Green River. The canyon is approximately 80 miles long and is known for its rugged beauty, with towering red rock formations and deep gorges. Desolation Canyon is a popular destination for whitewater rafting and kayaking, with numerous rapids ranging from Class I to Class III. The river also offers opportunities for fishing and hiking, with several trails leading to scenic viewpoints and historic sites. Gray Canyon, on the other hand, is located further west in Utah, near the town of Green River. It is a deep canyon that has been carved by the Green River over millions of years. The canyon is approximately 40 miles long, and it offers stunning views of towering cliffs, rock formations, and the river itself.

Grand Canyon Arizona

The Grand Canyon is a world-renowned natural wonder located in northern Arizona, United States. It is a massive canyon, approximately 277 miles (446 km) long, up to 18 miles (29 km) wide, and up to 6,000 feet (1,800 meters) deep, carved by the Colorado River over millions of years. It offers breathtaking views of colorful rock formations, steep cliffs, and the winding Colorado River at the bottom of the canyon. Visitors can explore the canyon through a variety of activities, including hiking, rafting, camping, and helicopter tours. The Grand Canyon is managed by the National Park Service and is one of the most visited national parks in the United States. It is considered one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World and is a popular destination for domestic and international travelers alike.

Intinerary

Day 1:
1° Arrive at the starting point of the river tour and meet your guide
2° Receive a safety briefing and instructions on the raft
3° Set off on the river and navigate through calm sections of the water
4° Reach the first set of rapids and work together
5° Set up camp for the night on a designated campsite
6° Enjoy dinner and campfire with your group

Day 2:
1° Wake up early and enjoy breakfast before setting off on the river
2° Encounter more challenging rapids and work together with your group
3° Stop for a picnic lunch along the riverbank
4° Continue down the river and enjoy the scenery
5° Reach the end point of the river tour and disembark from the raft
6° Say goodbye to your guide and fellow rafters
